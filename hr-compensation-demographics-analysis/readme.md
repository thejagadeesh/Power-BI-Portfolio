## Project Overview
This project provides a strategic deep-dive into the organization's human capital, focusing on **headcount growth trends**, **gender-based compensation equity**, and the **correlation between education and salary**. The dashboard acts as a tool for HR leadership to ensure fair pay practices and plan for future workforce expansion.
![--](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/andreasbm/readme/master/assets/lines/rainbow.png)

## Objective
The primary goal was to analyze employee demographics and salary distributions to identify potential biases and monitor the company's scaling progress from 2018 to the present.
![--](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/andreasbm/readme/master/assets/lines/rainbow.png)

## 🛠️ Tools & Technologies
* **Power BI** – Interactive reporting and data visualization.
* **Power Query** – ETL processes to clean and categorize employee qualification data.
* **DAX** – Advanced measures for Average Salary, Cumulative Headcount, and Gender Pay ratios.

![--](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/andreasbm/readme/master/assets/lines/rainbow.png)

## 💡 Key Insights
* **Headcount Scaling:** Sustained growth since 2018, with the workforce reaching **161 employees**, showing a successful long-term hiring strategy.
* **Gender Pay Equity:** Strong compensation fairness across the organization, with **Females ($55.37K)** and **Males ($52.86K)** earning comparable average salaries.
* **Education Premium:** There is a clear financial ROI for education; **Master's Degree** holders earn the highest average salary at **$67.04K**, significantly higher than those with a High School diploma ($48.9K).
* **Workforce Maturity:** The majority of the workforce (85 employees) is in the **30-40 age bracket**, representing a stable, mid-career talent pool.
* **Departmental Distribution:** Packaging and Production roles represent the largest employee segments by job title.
  
![--](https://raw.githubusercontent.com/andreasbm/readme/master/assets/lines/rainbow.png)

## Business Recommendations
1. **Sustain Pay Equity:** Continue monitoring the gender pay gap as the company scales to ensure current fair-pay standards remain a core part of the culture.
2. **Education Incentives:** Since higher qualifications correlate with higher compensation, implement a tuition reimbursement program to encourage current employees to upskill.
3. **Succession Planning:** With a dense population in the 30-40 age range, HR should focus on internal leadership pipelines to prepare for future senior management needs.
